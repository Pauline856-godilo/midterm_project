Why: A mix of character types increases complexity, making the password harder to guess. 3. Avoid Common Words and Patterns Avoid: Common words like password, admin, or 123456. Predictable patterns like qwerty, abcd1234, or 111111. Why: Hackers use dictionaries and common patterns to guess passwords.

4. Use Passphrases Example: MyDogLoves2Eat@Cheese! Why: Passphrases are easier to remember and harder to crack than random strings.

5. Avoid Personal Information Avoid: Names, birthdays, phone numbers, or addresses. Why: Personal information is easy to guess or find online.

6. Use Unique Passwords for Each Account Why: If one account is compromised, others remain secure.
